About

I am a Ph.D. graduate from the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology Madras. My research focuses on the design and analysis of scalable, fault-tolerant distributed systems with an emphasis on blockchain protocols and randomized communication processes.

Research Interests

  • Distributed Algorithms and Protocols
  • Blockchain Systems and Consensus
  • Randomized & Gossip-based Algorithms
  • Byzantine Fault Tolerance
  • Peer-to-Peer Networks
  • Performance Evaluation of Large-Scale Systems

Doctoral Research

Blockene: Scalable Blockchain with Server-Assisted Agreement

Designed and analyzed Blockene, a blockchain protocol where a set of servers assist in running the blockchain while tolerating Byzantine behavior. The work studies correctness, throughput, and scalability under varying fractions of faulty servers.

COBRA Walks on Graphs

Studied COBRA (Coalescing–Branching Random Walk) processes for information dissemination. Analyzed cover time, message complexity, and behavior on different graph topologies including complete graphs, trees, and expanders.

Experimental Evaluation

Built large-scale simulations and system prototypes using Node.js, libp2p, Prometheus, and Docker/Kubernetes to evaluate performance under adversarial and asynchronous environments.
